SMC-22 Festival
June 7-11, 2022
Saint-Etienne
(France)

ABOUT THE SMC-22 FESTIVAL

The artistic program of SMC-22 will take the form of a 5 days festival on June 7-11, 2022. Our goal is to open it to all forms of arts and genres of music involving technology. It will be open to the public with many free concerts that will take place in a broad range of venues throughout the city of Saint-Étienne. Each venue will target a specific setting: club, seated concert, orchestral music, spatialized music, electro/pop/rock, etc. Pieces, groups, artists, installations, etc. will be selected through a "Call for Art" that will be launched in early Fall 2021. One of the specificity of this call will be to give extended information about the spaces and venues that will be available for the SMC-22 festival as well the various kinds of equipment, musicians, and ensembles (i.e., orchestras, chamber music, etc.) that will be mobilized for this event.

CALL FOR ART

The SMC-22 Call for Art will be launched in early Fall 2021. It will provide extended technical information about the various venues and spaces that will be available during SMC-22. Students from Saint-Étienne conservatory will be mobilized to provide performers for some of the pieces that will be performed as part of the festival. A special "contest" will also be launched to have a piece performed by the Ensemble Orchestral Contemporain (EOC) during a concert that will take place on June 9, 2022 at the Théâtre Copeau of Saint-Étienne Opera House.

THE COMÈTE

The Comète is a space dedicated to artistic creation at the heart of Saint-Étienne. Its 650 seats concert hall will host a concert as part of the SMC-22 festival on June 7, 2022 as well as the inauguration coktail of the scientific conference. The venue will be equiped with an ambisonic system and the concert will feature pieces by members of the SMC community selected through the SMC-22 Call for Art.

THE CORBUSIER CHURCH

The Saint-Pierre church was designed by Le Corbusier in 1973. Beside its exceptional architecture, this space features an incredibly long reverb time (over 14 seconds). A concert will take place there as part of the SMC-22 festival on June 8, 2022. Pieces involving small ensembles and potentially taking advantage of the acoustical properties of the space will be priviledged.

THE THÉÂTRE COPEAU

The Théâtre Copeau is a 300 seats concert hall part of Saint-Étienne Opera House. It will host a concert during SMC-22 by the Ensemble Orchestral Contemporain (EOC). This concert will talke place on June 9, 2022 and will feature pieces by students from Saint-Étienne conservatory, members of the SMC-22 community (through the SMC-22 Call for Art), and from the EOC standard repertoire.

THE FIL

The FIL is a venue targetting a wide range of musical styles: rock, pop, jazz, electro, etc. Its main room can hosts an audience of 1200 people and its "club" about 300 people. The last concert of SMC-22 will take place at the FIL on June 11, 2022. The early part of the evening will happen in the club (which also hosts a bar) and will focus on SMC-22 experimental live performances. The second part of the evening which will start after 10:30pm will happen in the main room and will focus on experiemental electronic music by members of the SMC-22 community and will also feature a headliner which has yet to be determined.

THE BOURSE DU TRAVAIL

The industrial revolution had a huge impact in Saint-Étienne. The various coal mines and factories constituted a huge pool of workers and unions played a huge role in that context. The bourse du travail is the home of unions in Saint-Étienne. This beautiful building dates back from the beginning of the 20th century and often hosts concerts. SMC-22 late night concerts will take place there on June 8, 9, and 10th, 2022. Unlike on the picture you see on the right, these concerts will happen in a standing/club setting. An eight channels sound system will be installed in this space where pieces involving a "beat" and selected through the SMC-22 Call for Art will be performed.
